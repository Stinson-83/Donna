03 · Patterns & Voice

The way she behaves.

Foundations tell you what exists. Components tell you how each part is built. This page tells you when to use which · the logic that keeps every screen feeling like the same product.

Text hierarchy · what to use where

A decision chart, not a style guide. If a screen feels unclear, it’s almost always because two levels are competing, or the right level was skipped.

Display A quieter AI. Hero of cover/landing. Once per marketing page, never in-product.
H1 · Page title Good morning, Arnav. One per page. The first thing read. Personal when possible.
H2 · Section Today, gently. Introduces a chapter of content. Has space above, not below.
H3 · Subsection The things you cared about. Groups related cards. Never decorative · only when there’s a real grouping.
H4 · Card title This week with Maya. Default title inside a card or list item.
Lead A short intro, never more than two sentences. Top of a page or section. Explains, then steps aside.
Body Everything a user reads to do something. Running copy, descriptions, card body.
Small · Meta Quiet details · timestamps, counts. Supporting information. Never primary.
Label · Caps SECTION LABEL · 01 Category markers, nav sections, step numbers.

Color · when, and when not

The rule is almost absolute: everything is ink; rust appears once. If you feel the urge to add a second color moment, remove the first one instead.

Do
A quieter kind of AI.
One word in rust. The rest in ink. The accent earns attention because it is rare.
Don’t
A quieter kind of AI.
Entire headings in color. Kills the intent. The italic serif is already doing the emotional work.
Do
Primary action in rust because it’s the keeping action. Decline in ink.
Don’t
Rust on a destructive action. Green on a save. Two colors fighting. This is every generic AI app.
The one-moment rule

Count the rust on any screen before shipping. Zero is sometimes correct. One is ideal. Two is always wrong · choose.

Signal colors

Moss, amber, oxblood only on status indicators · chip dots, chip labels, a hairline under a row. Never as button backgrounds. Never as text inside prose.

Card rules

Cards are not containers · they are thoughts. One thought per card. If a card has two sections inside it, it should be two cards.

Surface vs. hairline

Surface card (#F0EAE4) for the primary content of a row · things to act on. Hairline card (paper bg, 1px border) for reference content · things to know. Never mix in the same grid.

Padding

24px on mobile, 32px on desktop. Inner title → body: 12px. Body → meta: 16px, divided by a 1px hairline.

Elevation

Resting cards have no shadow. A shadow means the card is briefly above the page · popover, hovered drag, a highlighted moment. When it lands, the shadow goes.

Radius

10px (--radius-md). 16px only for hero or modal surfaces. Never pill. Never 0.

Width

Cards come in thirds of the content width. Three across on desktop, one across on mobile. Never two-and-a-half · no orphan card sitting alone in a row.

Spacing · the rhythm

Every page has three spacing moments: between sections, between elements, inside elements. Keep them distinct. If the space between a title and its body equals the space between sections, the hierarchy collapses.

Page top

space-9 (96) from the top nav to the page title. space-8 (64) from the page title to the first section. This is the breath the user takes before reading.

Section to section

space-8 (64) minimum. space-9 (96) if the two sections are different ideas. space-10 (128) when crossing a chapter boundary · the equivalent of a new page.

Inside a section

Title to first element: space-6 (32). Between grouped elements: space-4 (16). Between different ideas in the same section: space-5 (24).

The single most important rule

Space belongs to what follows, not what came before. That means a section title is close to its content, and the space lives above the title. This is why Donna looks calmly composed instead of divided into walls.

Voice & copy

She writes the way a sharp, warm friend texts you · direct, observant, a little dry. She does not perform. She does not add exclamation. She knows your name but doesn’t use it unless it matters.

Do
Maya’s sister lands in 40 minutes. Do you still want flowers?
Direct. Specific. Trusts the user to remember context.
Don’t
Hi there! Just a friendly reminder that Maya’s sister is landing soon! 🎉
Overbearing. Performs warmth instead of being warm.
Do
Kept. I’ll nudge you Wednesday evening.
One-word confirmation. Then says the next helpful thing.
Don’t
Got it! I’ve successfully added your reminder to the system. You’ll receive a notification on Wednesday.
Talks about the system instead of to the user. Developer-tool voice.
Never

Em dashes. Exclamation marks. Emoji. The word “friend”. The phrase “I’m just an AI”. “Let me know if you’d like me to”. “How can I help?”

Names & pronouns

Donna is she. Not it. Not they. In-product copy never refers to her in the third person to the user · the user is speaking to her, so her voice is first-person.

Length

One or two sentences per message. If you need three, you’re telling, not helping. Break into a follow-up.

Illustration

Quiet editorial vignettes · small, restrained, thin-line drawings in rust ink on paper. Each one is an object, not a scene. A teacup. A pencil on a page. A key. Objects imply presence and memory without forcing metaphor.

the morning
something noted
a small key
the book
the thought kept
a note pinned
Style

Single-weight line (1.2–1.4px). Rust on paper only. No fills except occasional rust solid for a focal element. Rounded line caps. Gentle imperfection · these look drawn, not geometric.

When to use

Empty states, onboarding steps, email headers, marketing moments, section intros on the dashboard. Never as decoration inside a card. Never more than one per viewport.

Scale

Small. 80–160px. An illustration the size of your thumbnail, not a billboard. Restraint is the style.

Empty & loading states

Empty doesn’t mean blank · it means not yet. One illustration, one serif sentence, one optional action. Loading is almost invisible: a single rust dot, slowly breathing.
