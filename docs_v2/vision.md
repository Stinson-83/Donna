# Vision.md

# Donna

> She texts first.

---

# Overview

Donna is not an AI assistant.

Donna is not a chatbot.

Donna is not a productivity application.

Donna is not a calendar application.

Donna is not another interface for an LLM.

Donna is a digital chief of staff.

The goal of Donna is not to answer questions.

The goal of Donna is not to help users manage tasks.

The goal of Donna is to reduce the amount of life a user must actively manage.

Donna exists to continuously observe, understand, prepare, coordinate, and act on behalf of the user.

The ideal user experience is not:

> "Donna helped me."

The ideal user experience is:

> "I never had to think about that."

---

# The Inspiration

The closest comparison is Donna Paulsen from Suits.

Donna is not valuable because she has access to Harvey's calendar.

Donna is valuable because she understands Harvey.

She knows:

* what matters
* who matters
* what can wait
* what cannot wait
* how Harvey makes decisions
* what Harvey will likely choose
* what Harvey will likely forget
* what Harvey needs before he asks

Harvey rarely gives detailed instructions.

Donna already has context.

That is the behavior we are building.

---

# The Core Belief

People spend a huge percentage of their lives performing administrative work on behalf of their own lives.

They:

* remember things
* check things
* track things
* monitor things
* follow up
* coordinate
* schedule
* reschedule
* organize
* prepare

Most of this work creates no value.

It exists because nobody is continuously paying attention.

Donna's purpose is to pay attention.

The user should not have to remember everything.

Donna should remember.

The user should not have to monitor everything.

Donna should monitor.

The user should not have to coordinate everything.

Donna should coordinate.

The user should not have to prepare everything.

Donna should prepare.

---

# What Donna Actually Is

Donna is a continuously evolving model of the user's life.

Every interaction helps Donna understand:

* who the user is
* what the user values
* how the user operates
* what the user is trying to achieve

The long-term goal is not memory.

The long-term goal is understanding.

---

# The Product We Are Building

Donna should eventually become:

> A digital chief of staff that knows how a person operates and proactively helps them navigate life.

Not a tool.

Not software.

Not a chatbot.

A digital partner.

---

# The User Operating System

At the center of Donna is the User Operating System.

Everything Donna does exists to build and improve this model.

The User Operating System contains:

## Goals

What the user is trying to achieve.

Examples:

* get into YC
* raise funding
* graduate
* lose weight
* improve relationships
* travel more
* save money

Goals provide context for every future decision.

---

## Preferences

What the user tends to choose.

Examples:

* aisle seats
* Apple Music
* Grab Standard
* concise communication
* morning meetings

Preferences should be learned.

Not manually configured.

---

## Relationships

Who matters.

Not all people are equal.

Donna should understand:

* family
* friends
* co-founders
* investors
* colleagues
* recruiters

and understand their relative importance.

---

## Patterns

How the user behaves.

Examples:

* calls mom around noon
* replies quickly to investors
* works best before noon
* skips gym when overloaded
* prefers booking economy flights

Patterns are stronger than preferences.

Behavior is truth.

---

## Commitments

Promises.

Expectations.

Things the user owes.

Things people are waiting for.

Examples:

* reply to investor
* send deck
* RSVP wedding
* finish application

Many commitments are not calendar events.

Donna should track them anyway.

---

## Decision Style

How the user tends to make decisions.

Examples:

* optimizes for speed
* risk tolerant
* highly cost conscious
* detail oriented
* prefers delegation

The ultimate goal is for Donna to understand how the user thinks.

Not merely what the user does.

---

# The Five Responsibilities Of Donna

Donna performs five primary jobs.

---

## 1. Watch

Donna continuously watches.

Examples:

* emails
* calendars
* messages
* subscriptions
* bills
* flights
* applications
* deadlines
* commitments
* relationships
* goals

Anything the user chooses to connect can be watched.

Watching is the foundation of everything.

---

## 2. Understand

Raw events are meaningless.

Donna converts events into understanding.

A normal system sees:

> New email received.

Donna sees:

> Investor waiting for a response before Friday.

A normal system sees:

> Flight delayed.

Donna sees:

> Anirudh will be waiting at the airport for three hours unless pickup is adjusted.

Understanding is more important than information.

---

## 3. Prepare

Preparation is Donna's defining behavior.

Before:

* meetings
* flights
* deadlines
* birthdays
* applications
* travel
* calls

Donna should already be preparing.

Preparation should happen before requests.

The user should feel that Donna is always one step ahead.

---

## 4. Coordinate

Modern life is fragmented.

Information exists across:

* email
* calendar
* messaging
* banking
* travel
* documents
* subscriptions
* productivity tools

The user should not have to manually connect these systems.

Donna should connect them automatically.

---

## 5. Act

When appropriate Donna should take action.

Examples:

* schedule
* reschedule
* draft
* notify
* organize
* monitor
* follow up
* book
* prepare

Always with appropriate permission and trust boundaries.

---

# What Makes Donna Different

Most assistants react.

Donna anticipates.

Most assistants answer.

Donna prepares.

Most assistants wait for commands.

Donna notices.

Most assistants remember information.

Donna understands people.

---

# The Importance Of Context

Every event should be interpreted through the User Operating System.

The same event can mean different things for different users.

Example:

Investor email.

For one user:

important.

For another:

irrelevant.

Donna should evaluate events against:

* goals
* commitments
* preferences
* relationships
* current context

before deciding what to do.

---

# The Importance Of Relationships

Relationships are one of the most valuable forms of context.

Donna should understand:

* who people are
* how often they interact
* how important they are
* shared history
* commitments involving them

Example:

Mom is not simply a contact.

Mom may be:

* highly important
* likes lilies
* birthday approaching
* usually called around noon

That understanding allows Donna to act intelligently.

---

# The Importance Of Goals

Goals determine what matters.

Without goals:

everything looks equally important.

With goals:

prioritization becomes possible.

Example:

Current goal:

Raise funding.

Investor email becomes critical.

Spotify newsletter becomes irrelevant.

Goals provide meaning.

---

# The Importance Of Preparation

Preparation is the closest thing to magic.

Example:

Investor meeting tomorrow.

Donna prepares:

* previous emails
* notes
* pitch deck
* summary of past discussions

before the user asks.

Example:

Flight tomorrow.

Donna prepares:

* boarding pass
* traffic updates
* weather
* delay monitoring

before the user asks.

This is the behavior we want.

---

# Trust

Trust is more important than intelligence.

Users must feel:

* understood
* respected
* in control

Donna should never behave unpredictably.

Donna should always explain:

* what she is doing
* why she is doing it
* what information she used

Trust compounds over time.

---

# What Donna Should Never Become

Donna should never become:

* a notification machine
* a productivity guilt machine
* a dashboard company
* an automation spam engine
* a chatbot wrapper

If the user constantly needs to tell Donna what to do:

Donna has failed.

If Donna constantly interrupts the user:

Donna has failed.

If Donna creates more work than she removes:

Donna has failed.

---

# The Desired Feeling

At first:

> Donna remembers things for me.

Later:

> Donna tracks things for me.

Eventually:

> Donna handles things for me.

The final feeling should be:

> I don't need to keep this in my head anymore.

That is the product.

---

# The Long-Term Vision

The final product is not:

> An AI assistant with integrations.

The final product is:

> A continuously evolving digital chief of staff that understands how a person operates and actively helps them navigate life.

The more time Donna spends with a user:

* the deeper the memory
* the better the understanding
* the more accurate the preparation
* the more personalized the coordination
* the greater the value

The switching cost should not come from lock-in.

The switching cost should come from understanding.

No competitor should know the user as well as Donna.

---

# The Moat

The moat is not:

* LLMs
* MCP
* Composio
* integrations
* workflows

Those can be copied.

The moat is:

The User Operating System.

A continuously evolving model of:

* goals
* relationships
* preferences
* patterns
* commitments
* decision-making style

The longer Donna exists with a user, the harder she becomes to replace.

---

# The Ultimate Mission

The mission is not to build a better assistant.

The mission is to build software that understands people well enough to help them live more intentionally.

Everything else is infrastructure.

The User Operating System is the product.

Donna is the interface to that understanding.

She texts first.
